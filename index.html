About

The initiative for this project was spearheaded by Alex Stasuk, a seasoned IT professional with 13 years of experience and a member of the Supervisory Board of the Rivne IT Cluster. An extensive informational campaign was launched targeting the patients of the Rivne Regional Hospital for War Veterans. This initiative offers veterans the chance to receive specialized IT training in the following areas: a) Customer/Technical Support; b) DevOps; c) QA. The selection process, guided by the institution's staff psychologist, led to selection of 12 participants for the program. The ﬁrst session took place on August 20 in one of the hospital premises: Alex purchased the necessary equipment for all participants (laptops etc), prepared learning materials and brought pizza. The training adopts ﬂexible approaches, fostering a welcoming atmosphere with informal communication between participants and mentors. This encourages the exchange of both professional and life experiences. The sessions also incorporate shared pizza meals and walks during breaks, enhancing the overall learning environment. Training occurs both online and oﬄine, guided by a mentor. Weekly in-person sessions take place on Sundays for hospital patients, while online sessions are available for those outside the hospital. The mentor provides materials and supports independent study throughout the week. As of November 16, 12 sessions have been completed with 4 remaining participants. The foundational IT skills course spans 15 sessions, after which participants can choose a specialization - Support, DevOps, or QA - and proceed with an 8-10 week course. In order to improve English proﬁciency level, Alex engaged experienced online English instructors and organized personalized training for all participants. All project participants receive necessary legal and HR consultations. All developed instructional materials will be published under an open license as Open Educational Resources (OER) so that anyone interested can replicate the project in their regions.
